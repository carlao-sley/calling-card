Bauhaus Klassiker

This eight-page catalogue presents three pieces of Bauhaus seating furniture from the years 1919-1939. These include the Barcelona Chair by the master of modernism, Ludwig Mies van der Rohe, the Red and Blue Chair by the pioneer of the De Stijl movement, Gerrit Rietveld, and the Le Corbusier 2 by the architect of functionalism, Le Corbusier. It provides concise and compact information about the furniture and artists and, with its perspective layout, represents the typically clear, functional character of the Bauhaus.

Tu fais partie d'un tout

You often see the same gestures, the same people at the same times. Everything runs smoothly and follows the same pattern, day after day. Coincidence? Or something more complex, a system, a simulation in which we live and are unaware of because it runs quietly and silently in the background? Inspired by Nick Bostrom's simulation theory and George Perec's text ‘L'infraordinaire’, this book shows everyday life in Paris from a different perspective. On large picture and text pages, the banal, normal and boring are put in the spotlight and examined more closely.

Typografisches Liniensystem

This brochure presents the same graphic element in five different layouts. Readers can simultaneously enrich their theoretical knowledge and marvel at various practical applications. The cover can be unfolded and presents the line system in a reduced but clear form right on the title page. Designed by Swiss graphic designers in training, the brochure reflects the first systematic application of the line system by Swiss graphic design in the 20th century.
